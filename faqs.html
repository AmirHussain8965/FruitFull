Everything You Need to Know

Find answers to common queries regarding clinical trials, participant eligibility, and our rigorous safety protocols.

A clinical trial is a research study where new treatments, medications, or medical devices are tested to evaluate their safety, effectiveness, and potential benefits. Your participation helps advance healthcare for others.

Healthy volunteers or have the specific medical condition being studied. Meet certain age requirements. Fit other health criteria such as medical history, medications, or previous treatments.

Several safeguards are in place to help ensure participant safety:

  • Trials must be reviewed and approved by an Institutional Review Board (IRB), which ensures the study is ethical and that risks are minimized.
  • Participants are carefully screened for eligibility before joining a study.
  • A qualified physician and trained research staff monitor participants throughout the study.
  • Participants receive detailed information about the study through an Informed Consent document before deciding to participate.

After you sign up for a clinical trial, a member of the study team will contact you to review your health information and answer any questions. You may then attend a screening visit to see if you meet the study's eligibility requirements. If eligible, you'll review and sign an informed consent form explaining the study, its procedures, and potential risks. Once enrolled, you'll attend scheduled visits, be closely monitored by research staff, and may have follow-up visits after the study ends.
